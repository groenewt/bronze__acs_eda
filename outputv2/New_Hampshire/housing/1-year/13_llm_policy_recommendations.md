- **Trend Analysis**: By tracking changes in key variables over time (e.g., median household income), researchers can discern long-term trends and identify cyclical patterns or secular shifts.

### 3. Cross-Sectional Comparisons:

- **Comparing Urban vs Rural Areas**: Analyzing economic indicators and demographic trends between urban (e.g., Manchester, Nashua) and rural areas can reveal the differential impacts of regional development strategies on different socioeconomic groups.

### 5. Methodological Challenges and Limitations:

- **Data Quality and Bias**: Consider potential sampling biases or underrepresentation of certain demographic groups in PUMS data due to ACS limitations.
   
- **Cross-Sectional vs Longitudinal Analysis**: The choice between these approaches requires careful consideration, as each has its strengths and weaknesses in capturing dynamics over time versus comparing across years.
  
- **Causal Inference**: Establishing causal relationships amidst a large number of potential confounders necessitates sophisticated statistical techniques like propensity score matching or instrumental variables approaches.
