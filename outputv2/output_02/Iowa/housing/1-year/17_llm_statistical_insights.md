1. **Interpreting MEAN vs MEDIAN:** The Mean (calculated as the sum of all values divided by the number of observations) often represents a more central tendency for positively skewed distributions, while the Median (the middle value when ordered) is less affected by extreme outliers and thus often provides a better representation of the center for negatively skewed distributions. For instance, in income data, mean might be influenced by high earners pulling up the average, whereas median would show a more representative figure considering both low and high earners.

2. **Explaining SKEWNESS values:** Skewness is a measure of the asymmetry of the probability distribution around its mean. A negative skew (also known as left-skewed or leptokurtic) indicates that the tail on the left side is longer and/or fatter, meaning there are more observations with lower values than those with higher values. Conversely, a positive skew means the right tail is longer and fatter, indicating fewer extreme high values but possibly many low ones. The implication of skewness is that these distributions do not represent equally distributed data; they indicate an imbalance towards certain categories or outcomes.

3. **Interpreting KURTOSIS:** Kurtosis measures the "tailedness" of the probability distribution. A high kurtosis value indicates heavy tails or outliers, meaning there are more observations with extreme values compared to what is typically expected in a normal distribution. On the other hand, low kurtosis implies light-tailed distributions where most values cluster around the mean and only few deviate significantly from it.

4. **Discussing VARIANCE/STD DEV:** Variance measures how far a set of numbers are spread out from their average value, while standard deviation (STD DEV) is the square root of variance, providing a measure in the same units as the data. High variance suggests that most values deviate significantly from the mean, indicating high dispersion or variability among the dataset. This could be due to large differences between observations or outliers affecting the average value considerably.
